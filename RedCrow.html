USA STATEWISE DASHBOARD

DESCRIPTION

Kauffman indicators of Entrepreneurship is our base measurement tool that is calculated by the weighted average of four types of indicators. These indicators are the rate of new entrepreneurs, the opportunity share of new entrepreneurs, the start-up early job creation, and start-up early survival rate

RATE OF NEW ENTERPRENEURS

It is a measure of businesses created by a set of population in a state. It basically includes all kinds of businesses with no discrimination of its size, or the amount of people they're hiring, the amount of innovation they're doing, or its origin. We have taken average working hours of 15 hours per week per business to mark it as a running business.
It measures how many people are starting a business each month. A measurement out here can be understood as follows: a 0.14% in RNE means that 140 out of 100,000 have started a new business each month. The rate of new entrepreneurs in 2019 is 0.31.

OPPORTUNITY SHARE OF A NEW ENTERPRENEUR

This defines an intention for an entrepreneur to start a business. This can be either out of unemployment in order to survive or it can be out of curiosity to innovate.
This is calculated by the current population survey and takes the average of that particular year of that state and calculates the owners of new businesses and their opportunistic mindset. The opportunity shares of new entrepreneur in 2019 is 86.9%.

STARTUP JOB CREATION

It measures the number of total jobs created by new companies in their first year and then it's normalized by the population of the state. This is basically being calculated with the help of business employment dynamics. The indication of start-up job creation in 2019 is 5.2 per thousand people.

STARTUP SURVIVAL RATE

It deals with the percentage of new businesses which are running and still active after one year of operation. The only problem with this indicator is that it doesn't indicate the long-term survival of a company but only indicates the first-year survival of a company.
The rate of survival of startup in 2019 is 79.7%. This is also calculated with the help of business employment dynamics.


CONCLUSION

Startup Survival Rate (ssr) - Massachusetts & Wisconsin
Startup Job Creation (sjc) – Florida, California, New York, New Jersey, Nevada & Utah
Rate of New Enterpreneurs (rne) – Montana, Alaska, New Mexico & Colorado
Opportunity Shares of New Enterpreneurs (ose) – Delaware, Nebraska & South Dakota

KESE - WALLETHUB INNOVATION DASHBOARD

KESE INDEX

The Kauffman index, or the KESE index, is centered at 0 which is like an average of all the years’ data set that we have. We evaluate the weighted average and then normalize the values of all indicators present above to create a score.
The values about zero indicates the positive growth of the startup indicators as a whole, and the value below 0 indicates a low value of the index which is a below average performance. The index had the lowest value in 2009 which was -1.9.

WALLETHUB STATE INNOVATION INDEX

This Index ranks states according to 24 key indexes which are categorized into two ranks “Human Capital” and “Innovation Environment”.
Human Capital - (Total Points: 50) : Share of STEM Professionals (~14.29 Points), Share of Science & Engineering Graduates (~7.14 Points), Projected STEM-Job Demand by 2020 (~7.14 Points), Scientific-Knowledge Output (~7.14 Points), Eighth-Grade Math & Science Performance (~7.14 Points), AP Exam Participation (~7.14 Points)
Innovation Environment – Total Points: 50 : Share of Technology Companies (~4.76 Points), R&D Spending per Capita (~4.76 Points), R&D Intensity (~2.38 Points), Invention Patents per Capita (~2.38 Points), IP Services Exports as a Share of All Services Exports (~2.38 Points), Business Churn (~2.38 Points), Jobs in New Companies (~4.76 Points), Net Migration (~2.38 Points), Entrepreneurial Activityt (~2.38 Points), Number of Startups “Accelerated" per Total Number of Start-ups (~2.38 Points), Tax-Friendliness (~4.76 Points), Venture-Capital Funding per Capita (~2.38 Points), Average Annual Federal Small-Business Funding per GDP (~2.38 Points), Industry-Cluster Strength (~2.38 Points), Open Roads & Skies Friendly Laws (~1.19 Points), Average Internet Speed (~2.38 Points), Share of Households with Internet Access (~2.38 Points), Adoption of K–12 Computer Science Standards (~1.19 Points)

INDUSTRY WISE RISK ANALYSIS

DESCRIPTION

From this dashboard, we will be analyzing the risk involved with industries.
We try to see the average rates of all these indicators and how well are the sectors doing considering indicators. We generally keep the market risk or rather say the systematic risk as one; and hence beta value changes and fluctuates according to how the company or the industry is actually doing in the market.

AVERAGE UNLEVERED BETA

The average beta levered or unlevered, involves taking out the debt parameters before evaluating the portfolio of investment risk within comparison to the market.
Leveraged Beta / 1 + ((1 - Tax Rate) * (Total debt/Total Equity))
Unlevered Beta is directly proportional to cost of Equity

COST OF EQUITY

Returns an industry return an industry pace to its equity investors to compensate for the risk.
Cost of equity = Risk free rate of return + Beta × (market rate of return – risk free rate of return)
We kept the systematic risk as one; and hence beta value changes and fluctuates according to how the company or the industry is actually doing in the market.

PE RATIO

A higher PE ratio of an industry will be at low risk and also at a monitory risk reinvestment, where in a company or an investor is basically willing to pay if the company will own profits and build its market capitalization.

PEG RATIO

An amalgamation of PE ratio and expected growth rate. According to PEG ratio, the top industries with higher PEG ratio are often regulated or have major involvements of government, they are metal and mining, oil and gas, and chemical industries.
